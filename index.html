About Me

Hi there! Thank you for taking the time to visit. Here is a short glimpse into my story.

I am Peterson Yuhala, a PhD candidate and teaching assistant at the University of Neuchâtel, Switzerland. I conduct research in the field of distributed systems, more specifically on privacy preserving computation with trusted hardware technologies and persistent memory (PM). These have many applications in machine learning, blockchain technologies, and secure big data analytics as a whole.

I obtained a Master's degree in computer engineering from the National Advanced School of Engineering, Yaounde, Cameroon in 2018, and since then have been focused on research.

I am an electronics hobbyists and a great fan of Arduinos, Raspberry Pis and all the interesting projects that come with it.
